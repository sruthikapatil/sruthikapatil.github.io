Soybean Harvesting Tips

Here are some things to consider when harvesting soybeans:

1. When to Harvest

  • Soybeans are ready to harvest when the leaves turn brown and fall off, exposing the matured pods.
  • The ideal time to harvest is when the pods are pale yellow and the moisture content is around 13%.
  • Harvesting too early can result in immature beans, while harvesting too late can lead to shattering losses.

2. Harvesting Equipment

  • Combines are used to harvest soybeans.
  • The header on the front of the combine cuts and collects the plants, and the combine separates the soybeans from the pods and stems.
  • The combine operator then empties the soybeans into a grain truck or wagon.

3. Harvesting Tips

  • Drive slowly, at a speed of 3–4 miles per hour.
  • Set the concave clearance and cylinder speed to avoid cracking the seeds.
  • Synchronize the reel speed and ground travel speed to avoid cutter-bar shatter.
  • Cut as low to the ground as possible to capture low pods.
  • Check for losses on the ground.
  • Use an air reel to keep the knife cleaner.

4. Soybean Storage

  • Most grain elevators won't accept soybeans with a moisture content above 13%.
  • If the moisture content is too low, there will be increased shatter loss, splits, and weight loss.

Pro Tip: Always monitor the moisture content and aim for optimal harvest timing to avoid losses!
