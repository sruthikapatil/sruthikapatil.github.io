Here are some things to consider when harvesting soybeans:
1. When to harvest-
* Soybeans are ready to harvest when the leaves turn brown and fall off,exposing the matured pods.s
* The ideal time to harvest is when the podsare pale yellow and the moisture content is around 13%.
* Harvesting too early can result in immature beans, while harvesting too late can lead to shattering losses.
2. Harvesting equipment-
* Combines are used to harvest soybeans. s
*The header on the front of the combine cuts and collects the plants, and the combine separates thes
soybeans from the pods and stems.
* The combine operator then empties the soybeans into a grain truck or wagon.

* Drive slowly, at a speed of 3â€“4 miles per hour.
* Set the concave clearance and cylinder speed to avoid cracking the seeds.
* Synchronize the reel speed and ground travel speed to avoid cutter-bar shatter.
* Cut as low to the ground as possible to capture low pods.
* Check for losses on the ground.
* Use an air reel to keep the knife cleaner.
4. Soybean storage-
* Most grain elevators won't accept soybeans with a moisture content above 13%.
