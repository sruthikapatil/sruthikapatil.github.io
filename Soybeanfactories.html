Soybean Oil Processing Steps

The steps involved in processing soybeans in a factory are:

  1. 1. Cleaning:

    • The first step is to clean the soybeans to remove foreign matter like stems, pods, and leaves.
    • This improves the quality of the oil and reduces wear and tear on processing equipment.

  2. 2. Dehulling:

    The soybean hulls are removed because they absorb oil and reduce the yield.

  3. 3. Crushing:

    The soybeans are conditioned, sorted, and crushed. The seeds are then flaked.

  4. 4. Solvent extraction:

    The flakes are treated with a solvent to extract the oil.

  5. 5. Refining:

    The extracted oil is refined to remove impurities through degumming, deacidification, decolorization, and deodorization.

  6. 6. Oil filtration:

    The oil is filtered. After the oil filter process, the next steps in soybean processing are to refine the oil to improve its flavor, odor, color, and stability:

    1. 1. Degumming:

      Removes phosphatides from the crude soybean oil to improve its physical stability.

    2. 2. Gums conditioning and neutralization:

      Treats degummed oil with phosphoric acid to convert non-hydratable phosphorus into hydratable form.

    3. 3. Bleaching:

      Removes contaminants such as color pigments, polycyclic aromatic compounds, dirt, traces of metals, phospholipids, and other impurities.

    4. 4. Deodorization:

      Removes numerous volatile components through a vacuum stripping process.

    5. 5. Packaging:

      The refined soybean oil is packed in pouches, bottles, cans, and containers of different capacities.
