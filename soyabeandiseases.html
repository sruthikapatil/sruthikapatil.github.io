Soybeans are susceptible to many diseases, including:
1. Early-season diseases-
* These include seed decay, seedling blights, and root rots.
* They are caused by fungi in the soil, such as Pythium,
* Phytophthora, Rhizoctonia, and Fusarium.
2 . Foliar diseases-
* These include bacterial blight, bacterial pustule, brown spot,
downy mildew, frogeye leaf spot, Cercospora leaf blight, and soybean rust.
3. Viral diseases-
* These include bean pod mottle virus and soybean mosaic virus.
* Symptoms include mottled, crinkled leaves, mottled seed, and lower yields.
4. Green stem disorder-
* This disorder affects the normal maturation of stem tissue.
* Symptoms include fewer pods, and leaves or petioles may remain attached to the plant.
5 . Some ways to manage soybean diseases include:
1. Crop rotation -
* Selecting soybean varieties that are resistant to local diseases
2. Cultural management -
* Chemical management, which may vary depending on the pathogen and environmental conditions
6. To control diseases use following steps
Here are some ways to control soybean diseases:
1. Plant disease-resistant varieties:
* Choose a variety that is resistant to the diseases that are common in your area.
2. Use fungicide:
*Apply fungicide to the seeds or to the leaves of the plants.
* Fungicides with multiple modes of action are best.
3. Rotate crops:
* Rotate soybeans with crops that are not susceptible to the same diseases,
such as maize or sorghum. This breaks the disease cycle.
4. Scout for disease:
* Regularly inspect your soybeans for signs of disease.
5. Manage weeds:
* Weeds can help spread disease, so it's important to manage them.
* Improve soil drainage: Install subsurface tile to improve drainage.
6. Bury plant residue:
* After harvest, plow the field to bury plant residue com pletely.
7. Keep records:
* Keep a record of when and how severe diseases have occurred in your fields.
* This can help you decide how to manage the disease in the future.
