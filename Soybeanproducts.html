Here are some products made from soybeans: Tofu A cooked soybean product that comes in firm and soft varieties. Tofu can be baked, broiled, grilled, fried, or eaten raw. Soy milk A whole soy food that may have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and heart-protective properties. Tempeh A fermented soybean product with a dense, chewy texture and a nutty, earthy flavor. Miso A fermented soybean paste made by combining soybeans, salt, and koji, a type of fungus. Natt≈ç A traditional Japanese food made from fermented soybeans. Edamame A type of soybean that is harvested when green, about 30 days before the pods turn brown and become dry. Soy nuts Soybeans that are soaked in water, drained, and then baked or roasted. They can be used in place of nuts. Soy sauce A brown, salty liquid condiment made by fermenting soybeans or breaking them down with acid. Soy flour A gluten-free flour derived from ground soybeans that can be used in baked goods like cookies and quick breads. Soybean products have many advantages, including: Bone health Soybean oil contains vitamin K, which helps control bone metabolism and synthesize proteins that maintain bone mass. Menopausal symptoms Soy contains phytoestrogens, which are hormone-like substances that may help reduce menopausal symptoms like hot flashes. Cholesterol Soy foods are low in saturated fat and cholesterol, and high in fiber and polyunsaturated fats, which can help lower cholesterol. Omega-3 fatty acids Soybean oil contains omega-3 fatty acids, which can help promote health and prevent chronic disease. Reduced inflammation Isoflavones in soy can reduce chronic inflammation, which may help prevent chronic conditions like heart disease, cancer, and diabetes. However, some people have concerns about soy products, including antinutrients, phytoestrogens, and genetic modification. However, evidence shows that soy foods are generally safe. If you're concerned, you can opt for non-GMO or organic soy products.
