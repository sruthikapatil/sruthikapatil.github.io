*Soybeans have many uses in India, including as a food source, animal feed, and a component of meat substitutes:
1 . Food:
Soybeans are a common source of protein in the Indian diet and are used to make edible oil, soy meal, soy yogurt,
and textured vegetable protein (TVP)
2. Edible oil: Soybean oil is used in food and industrial applications.
3. Soy meal: Soybean meal is used as animal feed, especially in the poultry industry.
4. Soy yogurt: Soybeans contain significant amounts of dietary minerals, B vitamins, and phytic acid.
5. Textured vegetable protein (TVP): TVP is a meat substitute that is popular in India.
1. Animal feed- Soybean meal is also used as animal feed.
2.Soya chunks- Soya chunks, also known as "Bari", are a vegetarian meat substitute made from defatted soya flour.
* Soybeans are also known for their nutritional content, which can help build and increase bone density.
* They may also have anti-diabetic, anti-inflammatory, anti-cancer, and other health benefits.
* Soybeans may also have health benefits, including:
* Lowering cholesterol: Eating soy foods can reduce LDL (bad) cholesterol by around 3 to 4%.
* Reducing risk of chronic diseases: Soybeans may reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease, stroke,
coronary heart disease (CHD), and some cancers.
* Improving bone health: Soybeans may reduce the risk of osteoporosis in women who have undergone menopause.
* Reducing menopausal symptoms: Soybeans may reduce menopausal symptoms such as hot flushes.
